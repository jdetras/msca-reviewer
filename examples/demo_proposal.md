# DROUGHTSHIELD — Allelic architecture of drought resilience in rice

**Researcher:** Dr A. Fellow  **Host:** PlantGenomics Institute (EU)  **Duration:** 24 months

## Abstract
Drought is the single largest abiotic constraint on rice yield. DROUGHTSHIELD will
map the allelic architecture of drought resilience across the 3,000 Rice Genomes
Panel and deliver field-validated marker haplotypes for breeding. The fellowship
trains the researcher in statistical genomics and positions them for an independent
group-leader career.

# 1. Excellence

## 1.1 Objectives and beyond the state of the art
**Objectives.** O1: build a multi-environment drought-response GWAS atlas; O2: fine-map
five priority QTLs; O3: validate haplotypes in field trials.
The current **state of the art** in rice drought genomics relies on small bi-parental
populations and single-environment phenotyping. This project goes **beyond the state of
the art** with a novel, ambitious pan-genome haplotype framework. **Research question:**
which allelic combinations confer stable tolerance across environments? **Hypothesis H1:**
epistatic haplotype stacks outperform single large-effect QTLs.

## 1.2 Methodology
A sound, detailed **methodology** integrates field physiology, genomics and machine
learning in an **interdisciplinary** design. We explicitly address the **gender dimension**:
smallholder labour in rice is strongly gendered, so trait priorities are disaggregated by
the gender of farmers. **Open science:** all code and data are released under open access
with a Data Management Plan ensuring FAIR data; preprints accompany each paper.

## 1.3 Training and two-way transfer of knowledge
**Training** covers statistical genomics, leadership and transferable skills. There is a
genuine **two-way transfer of knowledge**: the researcher brings field-phenotyping expertise
to the host; the host transfers pan-genome and ML capability — clearly **complementary**.

## 1.4 Supervision and integration in the host
The **supervisor** has an outstanding **track record** and commits weekly mentoring; the
researcher is fully **integrated** in the host **research group** with a co-supervision panel.
